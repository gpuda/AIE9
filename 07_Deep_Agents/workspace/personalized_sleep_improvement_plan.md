## üì± Dealing With Your Phone Habit

**Understanding the problem:** Your phone emits blue light that tricks your brain into thinking it's daytime, suppressing melatonin (sleep hormone) for 2-3 hours.

**Your replacement activities:**
- Keep a paperback book on your nightstand
- Try a gratitude journal - write 3 good things from your day
- Listen to sleep stories or calm podcasts (audio only)
- Practice gentle stretches or meditation

**Pro tip:** The urge to check your phone will be strongest the first few nights. This is normal and will fade within a week.

## üö© Warning Signs & Troubleshooting

**If you're still tired after 2 weeks:**
- Double-check room temperature and darkness
- Ensure you're getting morning light exposure
- Consider if stress, diet, or medical issues might be factors

**If you can't fall asleep at your new bedtime:**
- Don't lie awake in bed for more than 20 minutes
- Get up, do a quiet activity until sleepy, then return to bed
- This prevents your brain from associating your bed with being awake

**If you slip up:**
- Don't try to "catch up" by sleeping in or going to bed extra early
- Just return to your regular schedule the next day
- Consistency matters more than perfection
