# Alex's 2-Week Vegetarian Nutrition Plan

## Program Overview
**Focus:** Boost energy levels, improve sleep quality, support exercise recovery
**Diet:** 100% Vegetarian with anxiety-supporting nutrients
**Structure:** 3 meals + 2 snacks daily with optimal workout timing

## Nutritional Goals
- **Energy:** Complex carbs + B vitamins for sustained energy
- **Sleep:** Magnesium, tryptophan, and calming nutrients
- **Recovery:** Complete proteins for muscle repair
- **Anxiety:** Omega-3s, magnesium, and mood-supporting foods
- **Hydration:** 8-10 glasses water daily + herbal teas

## MEAL PREP STRATEGIES

### Sunday Prep (30-45 minutes):
1. **Cook grains:** Make quinoa, brown rice, farro for the week
2. **Prep smoothie packs:** Pre-portion frozen fruit and greens
3. **Overnight oats:** Prepare 2-3 days worth
4. **Chop vegetables:** Wash and cut vegetables for easy access
5. **Cook proteins:** Prepare tofu, hard-boiled eggs, lentils

### Daily Tips:
- **Morning:** Keep smoothie ingredients ready
- **Work:** Pack snacks in small containers
- **Evening:** Prep next day's breakfast before bed

---

## HYDRATION SCHEDULE

### Daily Fluid Goals (8-10 glasses total):
- **Morning:** 1-2 glasses water upon waking
- **Pre-workout:** 1 glass water 30 minutes before
- **During workout:** Small sips as needed
- **Post-workout:** 1-2 glasses to rehydrate
- **Throughout day:** 1 glass every 1-2 hours
- **With meals:** 1 glass with each meal
- **Evening:** Herbal tea instead of water 2 hours before bed

### Best Choices:
- **Water:** Primary hydration source
- **Herbal teas:** Chamomile, peppermint, ginger (evening)
- **Green tea:** 1-2 cups maximum (morning/early afternoon)
- **Coconut water:** Post-workout for electrolytes
- **Limit:** Caffeine after 2 PM for better sleep

## WORKOUT DAY NUTRITION TIMING

### Pre-Workout (30-60 minutes before):
- Light, easily digestible foods
- Natural sugars for quick energy
- Minimal fiber and fat
- Example: Banana with small amount nut butter

### Post-Workout (within 30 minutes):
- Protein for muscle recovery
- Carbs to replenish energy stores
- Example: Greek yogurt with berries, or protein smoothie

### All-Day Workout Days:
- Slightly higher calorie intake
- Extra protein throughout day
- More emphasis on hydration
- Quality sleep foods at dinner

## FOODS FOR SPECIFIC GOALS

### Energy-Boosting Foods:
- **Complex carbs:** Oats, quinoa, sweet potatoes
- **B vitamins:** Leafy greens, eggs, nutritional yeast
- **Iron:** Spinach, lentils, tofu
- **Healthy fats:** Nuts, seeds, avocado

### Sleep-Promoting Foods:
- **Magnesium:** Dark leafy greens, nuts, seeds
- **Tryptophan:** Turkey, eggs, cheese, milk
- **Calcium:** Dairy, leafy greens, tahini
- **Complex carbs:** Oats, whole grains (evening)

### Anxiety-Reducing Foods:
- **Omega-3s:** Walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseed
- **Magnesium:** Dark chocolate, almonds, spinach
- **Probiotics:** Greek yogurt, kefir
- **Antioxidants:** Berries, dark chocolate, green tea

### Avoid for Better Sleep:
- **Caffeine:** After 2 PM
- **Heavy meals:** 3 hours before bed
- **High sugar:** Evening snacks
- **Excessive fluids:** 2 hours before bed

## BUDGET-FRIENDLY TIPS

1. **Buy in bulk:** Grains, nuts, seeds
2. **Seasonal produce:** More affordable and fresh
3. **Frozen options:** Fruits and vegetables retain nutrients
4. **Batch cooking:** Save time and reduce waste
5. **Generic brands:** Often same quality, lower cost
6. **Grow herbs:** Easy to grow on windowsill

## TROUBLESHOOTING COMMON ISSUES

### "I'm Still Hungry":
- Add more protein to meals
- Increase fiber intake
- Check hydration levels
- Eat more slowly and mindfully

### "I Have No Energy":
- Check iron levels (especially for vegetarians)
- Ensure adequate B12 (consider supplement)
- Balance blood sugar with protein + complex carbs
- Review sleep quality

### "Digestive Issues":
- Increase fiber gradually
- Stay hydrated
- Include probiotics
- Chew food thoroughly
- Consider food sensitivities

### "Too Much Prep Time":
- Use pre-washed vegetables
- Buy pre-cooked grains
- Batch cook on weekends
- Keep simple backup meals ready
